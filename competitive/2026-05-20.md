## AI Insights

### 1. On-chain agent payments reshape the data supply chain economics
Apify's x402 (USDC on Base) integration represents a structural shift: AI agents are no longer just users of web data APIs — they are becoming autonomous buyers. When an agent can call `pay-and-run-actor` without human approval, the purchasing decision moves from procurement to the agent's reward function. This has three downstream effects: (1) usage patterns will become less predictable (agents optimise for task completion, not cost), (2) providers who support x402 gain access to agent-driven demand before competitors, (3) enterprise buyers will want per-agent spend visibility and caps before deploying. Novada should assess whether payment-protocol support is a 2026 or 2027 requirement, and watch whether BrightData or Firecrawl follow Apify's lead.

### 2. Exa's Singapore infrastructure buildout signals winner-take-most dynamics in neural search
Exa is deploying its $85M Series B into GPU cluster expansion (5×), a Singapore engineering hub for H200 infrastructure, and a broader web index refreshed every minute. The key competitive dynamic: building a high-quality neural search index requires capital, infrastructure, and compounding data network effects — the same barriers that protected Google. Once the index quality gap between Exa and keyword-scraping-based approaches exceeds a threshold, enterprise buyers will standardise on neural search for accuracy-sensitive use cases (legal research, competitive analysis, scientific literature). Novada's product roadmap should decide now whether to partner with / integrate Exa or build internal semantic search — the engineering investment to compete with Exa's index is substantial and growing.

### 3. Social media scraping is entering a regulatory inflection point
BrightData's new Facebook/Reddit/YouTube Podcast scrapers arrive as the Smart TV SDK controversy exposes how residential proxy IP pools are sourced. Combined with ongoing platform ToS tightening (Meta, Reddit's paid API rollout) and the BrightData–Smart TV Google/Apple pressure, the window for low-friction social data collection is narrowing. For enterprise buyers who need structured social data pipelines, the risk calculus is shifting: managed, legally-reviewed providers will command a premium over raw proxy access. Novada should evaluate whether a "social data with provenance and compliance" positioning is viable before the next regulatory wave closes the gap between current practice and what platforms/regulators will tolerate.
