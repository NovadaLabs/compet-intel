## AI Insights

### 1. Exa's $85M raise redraws the search-layer competitive map
Exa's Series B at a $700M valuation — backed by Benchmark, NVIDIA, and YC — signals institutional conviction that purpose-built AI search infrastructure is a standalone business, not a feature. With a 5× GPU cluster expansion planned and Cursor's `@web` already routing to Exa, the company is pursuing index scale + latency simultaneously. For Novada, the window to differentiate on search quality or retrieval freshness is narrowing: Exa has the capital to commoditize both. The highest-leverage response is vertical depth — domain-specific corpora or entity graphs that a general-purpose index cannot easily replicate.

### 2. Firecrawl's Spark models are a bet that proprietary AI inference inside the scraping layer wins
By embedding Spark 1 Fast / Mini / Pro directly into `/agent`, Firecrawl is collapsing the scrape-then-infer pipeline into a single priced unit. This means their customers pay per "answer" rather than per "page," which is a fundamentally different value exchange. If recall benchmarks hold (50% for Pro, 40% for Mini vs competitors costing 4–7× more), this will be price-competitive against raw LLM + scraping stacks. Novada should evaluate whether its own retrieval pipeline exposes a similar "answer mode" — otherwise Firecrawl's Spark tier becomes the default choice for cost-sensitive agentic workloads.

### 3. MCP distribution is bifurcating: active players vs conspicuous absentees
Four competitors (Firecrawl, BrightData, Exa, Apify) now operate active, documented MCP servers with free tiers, IDE integrations, and developer-facing tooling. The other four (Tavily, Crawl4AI, Oxylabs, Decodo) have no standalone MCP presence. This split maps almost exactly onto the enterprise-vs-developer go-to-market divide: MCP presence is becoming the primary discovery mechanism for AI-native developers, while enterprise/proxy-first players rely on direct sales. Novada must decide which camp it is in — absence from MCP registries is increasingly not a neutral position but an active concession of the developer acquisition channel.
