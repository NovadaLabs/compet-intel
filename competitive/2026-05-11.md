## AI Insights

1. **Firecrawl's Parallel Agents (v2.8.0) reframe the competitive question from capability to scale.** Running thousands of simultaneous /agent queries changes how enterprise buyers evaluate web data APIs. The question is no longer "does your agent endpoint work?" but "how many can you run, at what latency, and what's the per-query cost?" Novada's KR-2 targets a 50-site benchmark — the right format — but it doesn't exist publicly yet. A published benchmark, even preliminary, changes the conversation from "no data" to "different architecture."

2. **Apify's apify-mcp-server release (May 10) is a coverage-by-ecosystem play that Novada's depth-first approach doesn't match — and shouldn't try to.** Apify connects agents to thousands of pre-built scrapers via MCP; Novada's bet is a deeply integrated search + proxy stack. KR-2's 50-site benchmark should be framed as "novada-search + novada-proxy on any site, zero per-site actor maintenance" — not "we support 50 sites."

3. **Exa's day-10 silent integration failures are the most actionable near-term acquisition opportunity in today's intel.** Developers are at the peak of the switching cycle: diagnosed the problem, haven't switched yet. novada-search is a direct substitute for Exa's neural search endpoint. A "migrating from Exa" one-pager converts this moment into a distribution event. The window is days, not weeks.
