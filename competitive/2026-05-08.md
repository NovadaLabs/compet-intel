## AI Insights

1. **Exa's broken deprecations are a live switching signal — and Novada should be ready.** The May 1 deadline has passed. Developers running `use_autoprompt`, `highlights`, or consuming Auto relevance scores are seeing live errors in production. Exa 2.0 is technically stronger, but breaking changes on a production API create switching motivation that no latency improvement recovers in the short term. The opportunity for Novada is not to point fingers, but to be the search API with a stable, developer-respecting contract — and to be visible in the places (Show HN, LangChain Discord, r/LocalLLaMA) where frustrated Exa users will go looking for alternatives.

2. **BrightData's social scraper GA changes the "single-call" benchmark calculus.** Facebook profiles, Reddit posts, and YouTube content now first-class in BrightData's MCP means a developer can get web content, proxy-rotated requests, CAPTCHA bypass, and social media data from a single server. This is a genuine breadth advantage. For Novada's KR-1 benchmark (beat on ≥5/6 buying dimensions), the honest answer is Novada cannot win the social scraper dimension today. The strategic response is to win decisively on the dimensions where the integrated stack (search + proxy, MCP-native, agent-first) creates genuine differentiation — and to publish those comparisons before the benchmark date.

3. **Firecrawl's audio extraction is a 90-day watch item, not a 7-day action.** Adding audio to `/scrape` (+4 credits/request) is a directional bet that AI agent workflows will increasingly process podcast audio, speech transcripts, and video audio natively. Today's share of audio extraction in agent pipelines is small. But extraction APIs that already provide raw audio content will gain a distribution advantage when audio-native LLM workflows become mainstream. No Novada endpoint has audio extraction. This warrants a backlog item — not immediate action, but a deliberate "we've decided not to do this yet" rather than an unexamined gap.
