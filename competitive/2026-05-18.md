## AI Insights

### 1. Exa's capital advantage is compounding — retrieval quality will be hard to match on a budget
Exa's $85M raise at a $700M valuation translates directly into compute: a 5× GPU cluster expansion + tens-of-billions-of-pages index refreshed every minute. Paired with the Exa 2.0 latency tiers (Fast at sub-350ms, Deep agentic at 3.5s), the company is now targeting every latency/quality tradeoff simultaneously. No scraping-first competitor is building infrastructure at this scale. Novada's best counter-strategy is vertical depth over horizontal breadth — specialized entity resolution, proprietary freshness signals, or domain corpora that a general index cannot easily index.

### 2. The scraping-layer is bifurcating: "answer mode" vs raw data
Firecrawl's Spark models and Decodo's AI Parser represent the same directional bet: move the value-add from raw page delivery to structured, LLM-ready answers inside the API response. Apify's `mcpc` and ForgeServers push this further — intelligence servers rather than data servers. Competitors still selling raw HTML/proxy bandwidth (Oxylabs, traditional Decodo tiers) will face increasing pressure as developers pay premiums for pre-parsed, agent-ready output. Novada should evaluate whether to expose an "answer mode" endpoint that bundles retrieval + extraction + structuring into a single priced unit.

### 3. MCP presence is now a developer acquisition channel, not a nice-to-have
Four competitors (Firecrawl, BrightData, Exa, Apify) operate active MCP servers with free tiers and IDE integrations; the other four do not. In a world where Claude Code, Cursor, and Windsurf users install tools directly from within their editor, absence from MCP registries is an active concession of the developer-first funnel. BrightData's free-tier MCP alone may be generating more organic developer sign-ups than traditional content marketing. Novada's MCP presence strategy deserves a concrete decision — build, partner, or consciously deprioritize — rather than defaulting to absence by inertia.
